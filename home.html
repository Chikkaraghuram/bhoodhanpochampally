History of Bhoodan Pochampally Sarees

Bhoodan Pochampally, a town in Telangana, is world-famous for its intricate Ikat weave. Known as the “Silk City of India,” it earned national recognition for its unique geometric patterns and vibrant color combinations.

The name “Bhoodan” comes from the Bhoodan movement led by Acharya Vinoba Bhave in the 1950s, who started his land donation drive here. The village later became known not only for social reform but also for reviving traditional textile artistry.

Pochampally Ikat was awarded the GI (Geographical Indication) tag and continues to thrive through handloom clusters that empower local artisans. These sarees are now treasured across the globe for their elegance, comfort, and cultural depth.

Narsimha Sarees proudly sources and promotes authentic Pochampally weaves, ensuring that every thread you wear carries a legacy of heritage and pride.
